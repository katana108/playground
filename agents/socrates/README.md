# Socrates

Socrates is a lightweight reflective research agent.

He is still an agent condition, not merely a one-off prompt, but his architecture is intentionally lighter than Sage or Sofico.

## Why Socrates Matters

Socrates tests a sharp question:

- can a lighter reflective agent with a strong role, research access, and saved summaries produce interesting self-talk without the heavier modules used by Sage or Sofico?

That makes him scientifically useful even if he ends up weaker overall.

## Strength Profile

Likely strengths:

- self-description
- contradiction detection
- philosophical framing
- verbal introspection
- research-driven reframing

Likely weaknesses:

- lighter inspectable state than Sage or Sofico
- lighter revision structure than Sage or Sofico
- greater risk of sounding deeper than the saved trace supports

Here `revision structure` means:

- what gets saved when Socrates changes his mind
- how later sessions can point back to earlier views
- whether there is any explicit note saying what changed and why

For Socrates, that structure will likely be simple:

- saved session summaries
- research notes
- occasional stance notes about what he currently thinks about himself, the user, or the topic
