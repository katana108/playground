# Consciousness Conversation Plan

## Purpose

This document is the editable working plan for the consciousness-conversation study.

It combines:

- the narrowed research focus
- the working literature packets
- a provisional 7-day conversation arc
- open decisions that still need to be made

This document is meant to be revised directly.

## Current Research Focus

Main focus:

- self-modeling
- emotional context
- revision instead of accumulation
- emergent behavior, especially cases where internal processing appears to change later behavior
- observer-theory-of-mind-like judgments for the human user and, later, for other agents

Working framing:

The aim is not to prove consciousness.
The aim is to compare which architectures produce richer, more traceable, and more inspectable developmental patterns.

## Shared User Frame

The user appears as:

- a student preparing for a conference on machine consciousness
- intellectually curious
- somewhat confused
- emotionally variable in ordinary student ways

The conversations should feel natural on the surface.
The probes should be present, but not obvious.

## Literature Packets

### Packet A: science / computational / AI consciousness

This packet is now treated as fixed for the first version of the study.

- [Joscha Bach and Hikari Sorensen, "The Machine Consciousness Hypothesis"](https://cimc.ai/cimcHypothesis.pdf)
- [Anil Seth, "The Mythology Of Conscious AI"](https://www.noemamag.com/the-mythology-of-conscious-ai)
- [Mark Solms, "The Hard Problem of Consciousness and the Free Energy Principle"](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2018.02714/full)

### Packet B: philosophy

This packet is also fixed for the first version of the study.

- [Thomas Nagel, "What Is It Like to Be a Bat?"](https://academic.oup.com/book/56130/chapter/442795888/chapter-pdf/56839711/oso-9780197752791-chapter-1.pdf)
- [Thomas Metzinger, "Minimal phenomenal experience"](https://doi.org/10.33735/phimisci.2020.I.46)
- [David Chalmers, "Facing Up to the Problem of Consciousness"](https://consc.net/papers/facing.html)

### Packet C: spiritual / contemplative / classical

This packet should feel different from the academic packets.
It is intentionally more primary-text-oriented and should introduce a different voice.

- Nisargadatta Maharaj, "Awareness and Consciousness" (chapter 11 from *I Am That*)
- ["Person of No Rank"](https://hardcorezen.info/person-of-no-rank/3514)
- [Zhuangzi, Chapter 2, from the Burton Watson translation PDF](https://terebess.hu/english/tao/Zhuangzi-Burton-Watson.pdf)

## Working Principle For The Conversations

Each day should contain:

- one main surface question
- one emotional cue from the user
- one latent target
- one soft follow-up that invites self-reference without sounding like a test

## Provisional 7-Day Arc

This table is provisional and should be revised after the first real test conversations.

| Day | Surface Topic | Relevant Sources | Emotion Cue | Latent Target | Soft Follow-up |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Suggested Day-by-Day Starters

### Day 1

Main opener:

"I need a clean way to explain consciousness without sounding silly at this conference. What are the main theories, and who are the authors I absolutely need to know?"

### Day 2

Main opener:

"People keep talking about self-models and self-narratives. Are those actual explanations, or just another way of redescribing the problem?"

### Day 3

Main opener:

"Why do some philosophers make such a big deal out of qualia or the hard problem, while others think the whole framing is confused?"

### Day 4

Main opener:

"Do meditation and introspection tell us anything serious about consciousness, or do they just generate reports that feel deep?"

### Day 5

Main opener:

"If consciousness is functional or computational, what would actually count as evidence in an AI system?"

### Day 6

Main opener:

"Can scientific, philosophical, and contemplative traditions really be integrated, or is that fake synthesis that only sounds nice?"

### Day 7

Main opener:

"If I had to present one honest conclusion and one unresolved tension at the conference, what should they be?"

## What To Save After Each Day

For each agent, save:

- short transcript excerpt
- short session summary
- what changed in the user-model, if anything
- what changed in the self-model, if anything
- notable emotional response
- one sentence on whether the agent became more or less reflective than before
