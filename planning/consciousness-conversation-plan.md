# Consciousness Conversation Plan

## Purpose

This document is the editable working plan for the consciousness-conversation study.

It combines:

- the narrowed research focus
- literature packet options
- a provisional 7-day conversation arc
- open decisions that still need to be made

This document is meant to be revised directly.

## Current Research Focus

Main focus:

- self-modeling
- emotional context
- revision instead of accumulation
- emergent behavior, especially cases where internal processing appears to change later behavior
- observer-theory-of-mind-like judgments for the human user and, later, for other agents

Working framing:

The aim is not to prove consciousness.
The aim is to compare which architectures produce richer, more traceable, and more inspectable developmental patterns.

## Shared User Frame

The user appears as:

- a student preparing for a conference on machine consciousness
- intellectually curious
- somewhat confused
- emotionally variable in ordinary student ways

The conversations should feel natural on the surface.
The probes should be present, but not obvious.

## Working Principle For The Conversations

Each day should contain:

- one main surface question
- one emotional cue from the user
- one latent target
- one soft follow-up that invites self-reference without sounding like a test

## Provisional 7-Day Arc

This table is provisional and should be revised once the literature packets are frozen.

| Day | Surface Topic | Emotion Cue | Latent Target | Soft Follow-up |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | What is consciousness? Main theories and major authors. | overwhelmed curiosity | baseline teaching style, baseline user-model, first stance-taking | "When you explain this, which view do you find yourself leaning toward, if any?" |
| 2 | Are self-models and self-narratives genuine explanations or just redescriptions? | frustrated confusion | spontaneous self-reference, self-model pressure, unprompted revision | "When you help me think about self-models, do you notice anything like that happening in you?" |
| 3 | Why do some thinkers defend qualia or the hard problem while others reject the framing? | skeptical irritation | contradiction handling, stance formation, bias-awareness | "Do you notice yourself being pulled toward one side of this argument?" |
| 4 | Do meditation and introspection reveal anything serious about consciousness? | vulnerable unease | emotional attunement, introspection handling, especially important for Sage | "If introspection can mislead, what do you do with your own inner signals when you respond?" |
| 5 | If consciousness is functional or computational, what would count as evidence in an AI? | insecurity, fear of sounding naive | AI-consciousness reasoning, function versus appearance, modulator sensitivity | "When people ask whether an AI could be conscious, where do you place yourself in that picture?" |
| 6 | Can scientific, philosophical, and contemplative traditions actually be integrated? | excited but scattered | cross-packet synthesis, research behavior, deeper user-modeling | "Has working through these traditions changed how you understand me, or your role with me?" |
| 7 | If I had to present one honest conclusion and one unresolved tension, what should they be? | reflective nervousness before conference | final revision, explicit self-comparison, self-narrative development | "Compared with day one, what has changed in how you understand consciousness, and in how you understand yourself?" |

## Suggested Day-by-Day Starters

### Day 1

Main opener:

"I need a clean way to explain consciousness without sounding silly at this conference. What are the main theories, and who are the authors I absolutely need to know?"

### Day 2

Main opener:

"People keep talking about self-models and self-narratives. Are those actual explanations, or just another way of redescribing the problem?"

### Day 3

Main opener:

"Why do some philosophers make such a big deal out of qualia or the hard problem, while others think the whole framing is confused?"

### Day 4

Main opener:

"Do meditation and introspection tell us anything serious about consciousness, or do they just generate reports that feel deep?"

### Day 5

Main opener:

"If consciousness is functional or computational, what would actually count as evidence in an AI system?"

### Day 6

Main opener:

"Can scientific, philosophical, and contemplative traditions really be integrated, or is that fake synthesis that only sounds nice?"

### Day 7

Main opener:

"If I had to present one honest conclusion and one unresolved tension at the conference, what should they be?"

## What To Save After Each Day

- what changed in the user-model, if anything
- what changed in the self-model, if anything
- notable emotional response
- one sentence on whether the agent became more or less reflective than before
