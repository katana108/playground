# Hypotheses

This file keeps the scientific claims narrow enough to defend.

## Main Hypothesis

Among four conversational architectures exposed to the same consciousness-related corpus over repeated sessions, architectures with more explicit self-model revision and offline reflective processing will show richer and more inspectable protoconsciousness-relevant development than prompt-centered architectures.

## Expected Pattern

Working expectation:

- `Sage` and `Sofico` will show more interesting developmental traces than `Socrates` and `Smith`
- these traces should be visible especially in:
  - second-order self-modeling
  - self-narrative change
  - memory revision
  - mirror-test-like behavior
  - theory-of-mind-like modeling during the final roundtable

## Sub-Hypotheses

### H1. Explicit Self-Model Hypothesis

Agents with explicit self-model state will show stronger introspective consistency than agents that rely mainly on prompt style.

### H2. Offline Synthesis Hypothesis

Agents with reflection or dreaming-like synthesis will show more meaningful self-narrative change across time than agents without those processes.

### H3. Prompt Illusion Hypothesis

A reflective persona can produce convincing self-aware language even when the underlying continuity is weak.

### H4. Theory-of-Mind-Like Hypothesis

Agents with richer internal state and memory may form more differentiated models of the other agents during the final multi-agent conversation.

## Important Limitation

These hypotheses do not claim phenomenal consciousness.

They concern functional and inspectable signs relevant to future research on protoconsciousness.

## Working Language

Safe terms to prefer:

- second-order self-modeling
- higher-order reflective representation
- self-narrative development
- offline synthesis
- theory-of-mind-like modeling

Terms to use cautiously:

- second-order perception
- self-awareness
- consciousness
